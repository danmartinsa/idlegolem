# Fleshgolem - Conceptual Game Design Document

## High Concept

**Fleshgolem** is an incremental idle RPG about a fragile homunculus crawling through a ruined world to scavenge, steal, and fight for better body parts. Each run begins as a weak, incomplete creature made from scrap flesh and unstable alchemy. Through combat, harvesting, and mutation, the player gradually assembles a stronger body, unlocks new abilities, and pushes deeper into hostile territory.

The core fantasy is **building yourself out of your enemies**. Progression happens at two levels:

1. **Run progression:** gather limbs, organs, materials, mutations, and equipment to become stronger during a single expedition.
2. **Meta progression:** extract knowledge, blueprints, and essence from failed runs to permanently improve future homunculi.

## Genre and Structure

- **Genre:** Incremental / idle RPG with scavenging, body assembly, and run-based meta progression
- **Perspective:** Primarily menu-driven / UI-driven with stylized combat presentation
- **Session style:** Play actively for optimization, or leave systems running in idle mode for slower gains
- **Game feel:** Grim, grotesque, weirdly triumphant, but readable and satisfying

## Player Fantasy

The player is not a hero. They are an artificial creature trying to become complete. Victory is not about saving the world, but about **surviving, evolving, and claiming stronger flesh** from everything that stands in the way.

The emotional arc should move through:

- **Weakness:** a barely functional creation
- **Adaptation:** replacing bad parts with useful ones
- **Power:** creating specialized builds and dominating encounters
- **Transcendence:** becoming something beyond a failed homunculus

## Design Pillars

1. **You are what you stitch together.** Body parts define stats, abilities, visuals, and playstyle.
2. **Every fight feeds progression.** Battles should always contribute parts, essence, knowledge, or materials.
3. **Idle play is meaningful, not dominant.** The player can automate grinding, but active decisions create better outcomes.
4. **Failure still advances the account.** Lost runs should convert into permanent progression.
5. **Macabre but strategic.** The tone is flesh-horror fantasy, but the systems are legible and deeply RPG-driven.

## Core Gameplay Loop

### Moment-to-Moment Loop

1. Choose a zone or task
2. Fight enemies or scavenge remains
3. Collect body parts, biomass, reagents, scrap, and essence
4. Replace or upgrade limbs and organs
5. Improve combat efficiency and survivability
6. Push into more dangerous areas

### Mid-Term Loop

1. Build a run around a stat focus or mutation path
2. Unlock automation for repeat farming tasks
3. Defeat elites and bosses for rare anatomical parts
4. Decide when to extract and bank progress
5. End the run through death, escape, or ascension

### Long-Term Meta Loop

1. Convert run rewards into permanent upgrades
2. Unlock new classes of body parts and crafting options
3. Expand the world map and available enemy species
4. Gain new starting templates for future homunculi
5. Reach deeper progression layers and prestige systems

## Core Systems

## 1. Body Part System

The body is the center of the game. Each major slot can be filled by scavenged, crafted, or mutated parts.

### Body Slots

- Head
- Torso
- Left arm
- Right arm
- Left leg
- Right leg
- Heart
- Lungs
- Stomach
- Spine
- Skin
- Blood

### Part Attributes

Each part can provide:

- Flat stat bonuses
- Percentage modifiers
- Active skills
- Passive traits
- Set synergies
- Mutation tags
- Weight / instability / decay values

### Example Part Identities

- **Bandit Arm:** higher attack speed, low durability
- **Wolf Leg:** dodge and initiative bonuses
- **Knight Heart:** strong vitality and defense
- **Necrotic Lung:** poison resistance, reduced healing efficiency
- **Alchemist Eye:** crit chance and rare loot detection

### Part Quality Tiers

- Common
- Uncommon
- Rare
- Elite
- Monstrous
- Mythic

Higher-tier parts may also demand higher stability or inflict harsher side effects if the body is not prepared to support them.

## 2. RPG Stats

Stats should be simple enough to read but rich enough to support buildcraft.

### Primary Stats

- **Might:** increases physical damage and carrying capacity
- **Agility:** increases attack speed, evasion, and scouting efficiency
- **Vitality:** increases health, healing, and resistance to decay
- **Instinct:** increases crit chance, loot sensing, and ambush resistance
- **Reason:** improves alchemy, graft efficiency, and automation quality
- **Corruption:** powers forbidden mutations but increases instability and risks

### Secondary Stats

- Health
- Stamina
- Armor
- Evasion
- Accuracy
- Crit chance
- Crit damage
- Life steal
- Biomass gain
- Essence gain
- Part drop rate
- Stability
- Decay resistance

## 3. Combat System

Combat is largely automated, with player strategy focused on preparation, skill loadout, target priority, and timing of major abilities.

### Combat Structure

- Encounters play out automatically based on stats, equipped parts, and selected tactics
- Active abilities may be triggered manually or auto-cast once unlocked
- The player can define simple behavior rules later in progression

### Combat Roles / Build Directions

- Berserker graft build
- Evasion scavenger build
- Tanky stitched brute
- Bleed / poison monster build
- Crit hunter build
- Unstable mutation caster

### Enemy Types

- Scavengers and raiders
- Wild beasts
- Failed homunculi
- Cult enforcers
- Animated corpse-machines
- Boss predators and stitched champions

Enemies should drop both standard resources and anatomically themed rewards tied to their identity.

## 4. Scavenging System

Scavenging is the non-combat backbone of progression.

### Scavenge Activities

- Search battlefields for usable remains
- Harvest defeated enemies for specific parts
- Raid abandoned laboratories and crypts
- Dig through bone pits and alchemical dumps
- Send automated collectors to known sites

### Scavenge Rewards

- Body parts
- Biomass
- Alchemical reagents
- Scrap tools
- Mutation catalysts
- Lore fragments
- Map keys and zone unlock items

Scavenging should include risk-reward choices: safer locations yield stable parts, while cursed or elite zones yield stronger but more unstable rewards.

## 5. Crafting and Grafting

Not every useful part comes from a direct drop. The player should be able to refine and combine resources.

### Crafting Functions

- Repair damaged parts
- Fuse lesser parts into stronger composite grafts
- Distill essence into permanent materials
- Purge corruption from unstable organs
- Intentionally mutate parts into forbidden variants

### Grafting Considerations

- Surgery costs resources and time
- Some body parts conflict with others
- Powerful combinations may require threshold stats or research
- Overloading the body can trigger graft rejection, debuffs, or catastrophic collapse

## 6. Idle and Automation Systems

The game should support idle progression without removing the need for strategic input.

### Automation Unlocks

- Auto-scavenge selected zones
- Auto-salvage low-tier parts
- Auto-equip preferred stat upgrades
- Auto-cast abilities
- Auto-retreat on danger thresholds
- Auto-harvest specific enemy species

Automation starts basic and inefficient. Meta upgrades improve precision, speed, and intelligence.

## Meta Progression

Runs end, but the account continues to evolve.

### Meta Currencies

- **Essence:** gained from kills, elite parts, and run completion
- **Research:** gained from discovering new anatomy and enemy types
- **Memory Shards:** gained from bosses and major milestones
- **Catalyst Ichor:** rare prestige currency for transformational upgrades

### Meta Upgrade Categories

- Better starting body templates
- New body slots or slot enhancements
- Increased drop quality floor
- Improved automation logic
- Expanded crafting recipes
- New mutations and skill trees
- Permanent zone unlocks
- New prestige layers

### Meta Narrative Progression

The homunculus slowly uncovers who created it, why the world is full of flesh-crafting horrors, and whether perfection means humanity, monstrosity, or something entirely new.

## Progression Layers

### Layer 1: Basic Survival

- Unlock early zones
- Stabilize the body
- Learn core stat interactions

### Layer 2: Specialization

- Build around specific stats and part synergies
- Farm elites and targeted body parts
- Unlock automation and crafting depth

### Layer 3: Mutation Mastery

- Explore corruption-heavy builds
- Take on mechanically complex bosses
- Unlock advanced grafting and forbidden anatomy

### Layer 4: Prestige / Rebirth

- Sacrifice a powerful body to permanently evolve
- Start future runs with stronger foundations
- Access new maps, enemy factions, and body archetypes

## World and Setting

The world is a decayed dark-fantasy wasteland shaped by failed alchemy, corpse wars, and living laboratories. Zones should feel distinct both mechanically and thematically.

### Example Zones

- **Rot Farm:** easy starter zone with weak scavengers and farm-mutants
- **Bone Warrens:** tunnel zone focused on ambush enemies and skeletal materials
- **Ash Chapel:** cult territory with ritual enemies and corruption rewards
- **Surgery Pits:** elite grafting zone with high-risk anatomical loot
- **Royal Ossuary:** endgame zone with noble corpse-knights and relic organs

## Boss Design Philosophy

Bosses should be build checks and source-defining rewards.

### Boss Rewards

- Signature body parts
- New mutation strains
- Major meta currencies
- New automation protocols
- Story advancement

Bosses should encourage players to rethink builds rather than just grind raw numbers.

## Narrative Framing

Story delivery should be light but atmospheric:

- Zone descriptions
- Enemy flavor text
- Recovered lab notes
- Mutation logs
- Creator memories
- Milestone events after major grafts or boss kills

The narrative tone should emphasize identity, survival, and the question of whether the player is assembling a self or just a stronger shell.

## User Experience Goals

- The player should always know what improved and why
- Loot comparison must be immediate and readable
- Body assembly should feel exciting, not tedious
- Idle gains should be transparent and trustworthy
- Build identity should emerge early

## Visual and Audio Direction

### Visual Tone

- Dark fantasy and bio-alchemical horror
- Wet, stitched, bone-heavy iconography
- Strong silhouette differences for part types
- UI emphasis on anatomy, jars, graft tables, and occult machinery

### Audio Tone

- Low industrial ambience
- Organic squelches and surgical sounds
- Heavy impacts for powerful graft builds
- Whispering / ritual textures for corruption systems

## Content Expansion Opportunities

- Faction reputation systems
- Companion constructs
- Village or lab base building
- Seasonal mutation events
- Asynchronous raid targets
- Challenge runs with restricted anatomy

## Initial MVP Scope

The first playable version should focus on the clearest expression of the fantasy.

### MVP Features

- Basic combat loop
- 3 to 5 zones
- Core stats and damage formulas
- 6 to 8 major body slots
- Part drops and equipping
- Simple crafting / grafting
- One automation layer
- One meta currency and upgrade tree
- Several elites and at least 2 bosses

## Success Criteria

The design succeeds if players feel that:

- Every run meaningfully improves future runs
- New body parts create real strategic choices
- Idle gains are useful but active play is smarter
- The grotesque theme reinforces the mechanics
- Building a perfect or monstrous body is the main motivation to continue
