# Game Design Document: FLESHGOLEM

**Project Name:** Fleshgolem  
**Genre:** Incremental RPG / Body-Horror Evolution Simulator  
**Theme:** Survival through biological scavenging and alchemical evolution.

---

## 1. Executive Summary
**Fleshgolem** is a dark-fantasy incremental game where you play as a sentient homunculus. You start as a weak pile of meat and must defeat enemies to scavenge their limbs, organs, and skins. By "stitching" these parts onto your own frame, you evolve, gain stats, and unlock powerful abilities to traverse increasingly lethal biomes.

---

## 2. Core Gameplay Loop

1.  **Slay:** Auto-combat encounters in various zones.
2.  **Scavenge:** Collect biological loot (Body Parts) and currency (Raw Meat).
3.  **Stitch:** Replace old or decaying parts with superior anatomical components.
4.  **Evolve:** Spend Bone Meal (XP) to improve base attributes.
5.  **Dissolve:** Prestige to reset your physical form for permanent Genetic Memory upgrades.

---

## 3. RPG Stats & Attributes

Your power is defined by five core attributes. These are influenced by your base level and the specific body parts you have equipped.

| Attribute | Biological Name | Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **STR** | **Sinew** | Increases Physical Damage and allows for heavier, armored limbs. |
| **VIT** | **Grafting** | Increases Max HP and reduces the rate of part decay. |
| **DEX** | **Reflex** | Increases Attack Speed, Dodge Chance, and Critical Rate. |
| **INT** | **Neural** | Increases Mana (Bone Marrow) and unlocks active skill slots. |
| **LCK** | **Mutation** | Increases the rarity of dropped parts and chance for "Fused" bonuses. |

---

## 4. The Stitching System (Equipment)

Rather than swords and shields, you equip anatomical parts. Every part has a **Freshness** meter; as it rots, its stats diminish until it is replaced or preserved.

### 4.1 Body Slots
* **Head:** Determines Brain Capacity (active skills) and sensory accuracy.
* **Torso:** The core. Determines HP and the number of "Internal Organ" slots.
* **Arms (Left/Right):** Determines weapon type (e.g., Bear Claws, Bone Blades, Tentacles).
* **Legs:** Influences movement speed between zones and flee success rate.
* **Internal Organs:** Hearts, Lungs, and Glands that provide passive buffs (e.g., *Acid Gland* for poison damage).

### 4.2 Alchemical Fusion
Players can combine two identical parts in the **Vat of Rebirth** to create a **Twisted Part**. These have random high-tier modifiers but cost "Raw Meat" to produce.

---

## 5. Meta-Progression: The Eternal Vat

When the game slows down, you may choose to **Dissolve**. You lose your current body and items but gain **Primordial Essence**.

### Permanent Upgrades (DNA Tree)
* **Hardened Marrow:** Permanent +10% Physical Resistance per rank.
* **Vestigial Echoes:** Start each run with a random Common-tier part.
* **Surgical Precision:** Increases the chance that scavenging results in a "Pristine" part.
* **Rapid Cellular Division:** Increases passive HP regeneration permanently.

---

## 6. World Zones
1.  **The Charnel Pits:** Tutorial area. Scavenge for Humanoid and Rodent parts.
2.  **The Fungal Wastes:** Combat mushroom-infested beasts. Introduces "Toxic" part types.
3.  **The Dragon’s Graveyard:** High-tier zone. Harvest ancient dragon bones and scales for extreme Sinew bonuses.
4.  **The Alchemist’s Spire:** Endgame zone. Battle failed experiments and gather "Eldritch" parts.

---

## 7. Visual & Audio Direction
* **Art Style:** Gritty 2D "Paper-Doll" animation. The character sprite changes visually every time a new part is stitched.
* **Palette:** Deep crimsons, sickly greens, and bone whites.
* **Sound:** Squishing, bone-cracking, and low-frequency atmospheric drones.
